Assumption Leads to a Misconception

We all know the term "Don't judge a book by its cover" very well, yet there are those who still don't follow this practice.

Posted by Kyla Jimenez on April 4, 2018

We all know the term “Don’t judge a book by its cover” very well, yet there are those who still don’t follow this practice. Though this saying is more commonly referred to a physical appearance of a person or object, the main focus right now is giving assumptions instead of understanding the truth. So, why is this being addressed in the first place? What is so urgent about it that it needs to be discussed in this post or article? Basing on assumptions is something that a lot of people, especially our generation today or possibly others, sadly do, which may create false rumors and create a misunderstanding. This needs to stop and how is it possible to even do so? It is possible to do this by not judging events or concerns at first glance, not believing rumors, and communicate or ask about the said concern.

First of all, we should not judge or base concerns on what we first hear or see. Judging first impressions is already a huge step on leading to an assumption. Based on experience, it is one of the causes of breaking relationships and starting fights that are fought for nothing but on trying to prove a false accusation true. For example, in the documentary Indolence, a Teenage Syndrome, the original idea of it was that the narrator, or the ones who made the said documentary, wanted to focus on how apathetic students are these days, and like framing theory, emphasizing that laziness and cutting out the fact the students, majority if not all, actually overwork themselves to the point they get into serious conditions. The narrator soon realized that her judgement on her first impression on how she thought how students these days act are not entirely true. How we can prevent ourselves from not basing people by their first impression is to simply not question it at first or decide as if we know everything. Everyone has their own problems to deal with, and we can’t just suspect that a person is doing something because of stereotype or what he or she is labeled to be.

Second of all, we should not believe all the rumors or gossip we hear just because it’s well-known or trending. Though it’s a human instinct to try and relate to others because we’re social beings, agreeing with an assumption means we’d also be assuming that it’s true as well. It is similar to, for example, how certain news companies take a trending topic but just a specific part of it and not give an entire explanation to why. This is sadly like the media today, they rather have commercialized reports for the sake of money rather than the sake of importance. What we should do to avoid this is to, of course, ignore rumors and, if concerned, look for the whole picture before deciding on an opinion. It’s better to understand the entire situation rather than to just side with something we do not know much of, just because it is popular.

Lastly, communication is key. If we don’t communicate, we won’t be able to understand a certain concern. According to an article made by Moore, lack of communication or an unresolved conflict can lead to misunderstandings, bad communication patterns or negative interactions, and deterioration or reduced relationship satisfaction. Going back to the documentary, again, the narrator presumed that the students were just slackers and contribute nothing to society. In actuality, they are dealing with their own problematic situations.
