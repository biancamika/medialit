Assumption Leads to a Misconception

We all know the term "Don't judge a book by its cover" very well, yet there are those who still don't follow this practice.

Posted by Kyla Jimenez on April 4, 2018

We all know the term “Don’t judge a book by it’s cover” very well, yet there are those who still don’t follow this practice. This relates to my main focus, which is about assuming instead of understanding the truth. So, why is this being addressed in the first place? What is so urgent about it that it needs to be discussed in this post or article? Sadly, basing on assumptions is something many do, especially our generation today. This can create false rumors and create a misunderstanding. This needs to stop, but is it possible? It is can be done by not judging events/concerns at first glance, being wary of rumors, and asking about the concern.

First of all, we should not judge or base concerns on what we first hear or see. Judging first impressions is already a huge step in leading to an assumption. Based on experience, it is one of the causes of broken relationships. Many fights are fought over nothing but a false accusation. For example, in my group's documentary "Indolence: The Student Syndrome", the narrator wanted to focus on the laziness of students. Like the framing theory, this was the only thing emphasized (e.g. sleepiness, starving). In reality, the students were actually "lazy" because they overworked themselves. The narrator soon realized that her judgement was not at all true. We can prevent ourselves from doing this by being considerate. Everyone has their own problems to deal with; we can’t suspect that a person is bad just because of a label.

Second of all, we should not believe all the rumors or gossip we hear just because it’s well-known or trending. Even if it's human instinct to be social, agreeing with an assumption means we’d also believe that it's true. For example, some news companies only depict specific part of a trending topic. This can be done without an explanation as to why. Sadly, this is what the media does today. They would rather have commercialized reports for the sake of money. What we should do to avoid this is to ignore rumors. If there's still concern, look for the whole picture before deciding on an opinion. It’s better to understand the entire situation rather than to side with a popular opinion.

Lastly, communication is key. If we don’t communicate, we won’t be able to understand a certain concern. According to Moore (n.d.), a lack of communication can lead to misunderstandings, negative interactions, and reduced relationship satisfaction. Going back to the documentary, the narrator presumed that the students were just slackers. In actuality, they are dealing with their own problematic situations.

In conclusion, it is actually pretty simple to not assume things. It is pretty sad to know that people these days don’t really realize this.
