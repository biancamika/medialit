The Impact of Technology on our Daily Mindset

What is technology doing to us?

Posted by Eliza Bumanlag on April 4, 2018

In our generation, every single person has device in possession. Laptops, iPads, mobile phones, television, even possibly an apple watch. However, haven’t you notice how much time we use on these gadgets every single day? According to a survey conducted by analytics firm Flurry, we’re up to 5 hours per day on our mobile devices. What is technology doing to us? Our generation is filled with the power of technology and seemingly, at some point, we unconsciously allow it to take control of our lives. Here are two situations of using technology almost every minute of the day with a bad affect: gamers having a lack of sleep and procrastinating by going on to social media.

Firstly, we will tackle the issue about gamers having the lack of sleep. Gamers spend their free time playing games and as for me, I spend about 2 to 4 hours playing games everyday. According to the Verto App Report (published February 2016), social media activity touches nearly all mobile app users (99%), while mobile games reach 57% of mobile app users. Games rank second behind social media activity in terms of time spent on devices. That equates to a whopping 1.15 billion hours that consumers spend each month playing games. However, students and working adults are usually the victims of having a lack of sleep. Due to the fact that school or work occurs during most of the day, gamers will tend to play games at night. We end up missing the time and start playing at 10pm and end up realizing its 2am when you finish. Furthermore, this could strain our eyes and cause harm to our health. Using gaming as an excuse for having no sleep is not right. We end up not doing our daily tasks properly and this could even lead to procrastination.

Speaking of procrastination, this leads us to our second situation. According to Asano (2017), Astonishingly, the average person will spend nearly two hours (approximately 116 minutes) on social media everyday, which translates to a total of 5 years and 4 months spent over a lifetime. Broken down, time spent on social media differs across each platform. YouTube comes in first, consuming over 40 minutes of a person's day (i.e. 1 year and 10 months in a lifetime). Facebook users will spend an average of 35 minutes a day, totalling 1 year and 7 months in a lifetime (some statistics include Facebook, Facebook-owned Instagram, and Facebook Messenger for total time spent on Facebook). Snapchat and Instagram come in next with 25 minutes and 15 minutes spent per day, respectively. Finally, users will spend 1 minute on Twitter, spanning 18 days of usage in a lifetime.With the daily usage of social media, we tend to distract ourselves from our daily tasks. For example, a student goes on Youtube instead and laughs at Vine compilations instead of doing her assigned homework . Unfortunately, her homework is due in two hours and this can cause the student to be stressed once they start on the homework late.

Sadly, there are even more different situations that affect our frame of mind and health just because of the impact of technology. Technology will never leave our hands at this point of time as it increasingly gets more advanced with more inventions occurring. We have to beware of the effects of what technology could do to us and, use technology to a safer extent.
